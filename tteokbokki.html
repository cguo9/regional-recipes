Tteokbokki (Spicy Rice Cake)

380 Calories, 8g Sugar, 800g Sodium, 6.8g Protein

Originating from South Korea, tteokbokki is a famous local street food that is spicy, sweet and chewy. This delectable dish usually consists of anchovies, fish cake, spicy chili pepper paste and of course, rice cakes!

Ingredients: (Serves 3)

Please beware of any allergies or any other medical afflictions you may have!

  • 1 Pound Korean Rice Cake
  • 7 Dried Anchovies
  • 4 oz Green Cabbage
  • 6 x 8 Inch Dried Kelp
  • 4 Cups Water
  • 1⁄3 Tablespoons Red Chili Pepper Paste
  • 1-3 Teaspoons Red Chili Pepper Flakes (Optional)
  • 3 Green Onions
  • 1 Tablespoons Sugar
  • 2 Hard Boiled Eggs (Optional)
  • 1⁄2 Pounds Korean Fish Cake (Optional)
    •

Instructions: (30 Minutes)

  1. Add the water, dried anchovies, and dried kelp to a shallow pot or pan.
  2. Boil for 15 minutes over medium high heat without the lid.
  3. Combine hot pepper paste, hot pepper flakes, and sugar in a small bowl. Remove the anchovies and kelp from the pot and add the rice cake, the mixture in the bowl, the green onion, and the optional fish cakes and hard boiled eggs. The stock will be about 2 1⁄2 cups.
  4. Stir gently with a wooden spoon when it starts to boil. Keep stirring until the rice cake turns soft and the sauce thickens and looks shiny, which should take about 10 -15 minutes. If the rice cake is not soft enough, add more water and continue stirring until soften. When you use freshly made rice cake, it takes shorter time. If you use frozen rice cake, thaw it out and soak in cold water to soften it before cooking.
  5. Remove from the heat and serve hot. Enjoy!
